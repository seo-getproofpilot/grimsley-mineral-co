### Strike Types

MS

Mint State

The numerical grades MS-60 through MS-70, used to denote a business strike coin that never has been in circulation. A Mint State coin can range from one that is covered with marks (MS-60) to a flawless example (MS-70).

PR

Proof

A Proof coin is struck with specially prepared dies and planchets, often with multiple blows for sharper detail. Produced at slower speeds and higher pressure, Proofs have finer detail than regular strikes. PCGS recognizes Proofs (PR) from 1817 onward.

SP

Specimen

Special coins struck at the Mint from 1792-1816 that display many characteristics of the later Proof coinage. Prior to 1817, the minting equipment and technology was limited, so these coins do not have the "watery" surfaces of later Proofs nor the evenness of strike of the close collar Proofs.

### Plus Grades

"Plus Grades" exhibit exceptional eye appeal for the grade and constitute the top 30% of the coins in the grade. Only XF-45 through MS/PR-68 are eligible for Plus grades. Numerically, this includes 45, 50, 53, 55, 58, 62, 63, 64, 65, 66, 67, & 68.

### Types

Holdered

No grade coins returned to the submitter with encapsulation. Within this category, PCGS assigns "Details" grades. These correspond to the amount of wear the coin has seen, but does not mean that the assigned grade can be used to determine the coin's value from published price guides. Generally, the coin's value will be less than that shown in price guides.

Not Holdered

No grade coins returned to the submitter without encapsulation.
