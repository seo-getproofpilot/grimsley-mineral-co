- Where can I buy replacement parts for my Mathmos lava lamp?







We offer a range of replacement parts for many current and recent models, including [bottles](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bottles/), [bulbs](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bulbs/), [bases, caps, and other small parts](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/bases-caps-small-parts/).



Mathmos lava lamps are designed to last, with replacement parts available for many models. If you cannot find the part you need, please contact our [customer service](https://mathmos.com/contact-us/) team.

- Can I exchange a replacement bottle that does not fit my lava lamp?







If your replacement bottle does not fit your lamp, please contact our [customer service team](https://mathmos.com/contact-us/) with your order number and details of your lamp. Subject to our [Returns Policy](https://mathmos.com/delivery-and-returns/), we can advise on exchange options.

- Why is there a metal spring or coil in my lava lamp bottle?







The metal spring at the bottom of your lava lamp bottle is an essential part of the lamp and helps the lava flow correctly. If the spring becomes dislodged, run the lamp for up to six hours to allow it to settle back into place. This may need to be repeated if the spring does not settle on the first attempt.

- What makes a Mathmos lava lamp different from other lava lamps?







Mathmos has been manufacturing high quality lava lamps in Britain since 1963. Our lamps feature a unique lava formulation developed to deliver superior flow, longer life, clearer liquid and more vivid colours. We also offer a comprehensive range of [spare parts](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/) to help keep your lamp performing at its best for years to come. Every Mathmos lava lamp is designed to meet the highest safety standards and undergoes regular independent testing to ensure exceptional quality.

- Why is my lava lamp forming stalagmite shapes?







Stalagmite shapes are a normal part of the lava lamp warming up process. As the lamp heats up, these shapes will gradually melt and transform into the flowing lava effect. Once the lamp has reached its optimum operating temperature, the lava should begin to flow normally.

- Why does my lava lamp look a different colour from the photos?







Mathmos lava lamp bottles are handcrafted, so slight variations in colour can occur. More commonly, the appearance of a lava lamp can vary depending on room lighting and screen display settings. Our product photos are taken to represent the colours as accurately as possible, but colours may appear different under different lighting conditions. For more information on how lighting can affect the appearance of your lava lamp, please see our blog article [The Appearance of Colour](https://mathmos.com/the-appearance-of-colour/).

- What is Mathmos doing about the halogen bulb phase-out?







Mathmos continues to use compliant halogen bulbs to heat and light its lava lamps, despite the phase-out of some halogen bulbs for general lighting applications. We are also exploring alternative technologies for future lava lamp designs should regulations change further. Our commitment is to keep Mathmos lava lamps in use for as long as possible by continuing to offer [spare parts](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/), [replacement bulbs](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bulbs/) and ongoing support wherever possible.

- How long does a Mathmos lava lamp bottle or bulb last?







Mathmos lava lamp bottles typically provide around 2,000 hours of operation, although many customers enjoy their bottles for much longer. The lifespan of a bulb can vary depending on usage and handling. Bulbs purchased directly from Mathmos are covered by a one year guarantee. Replacement [bottles](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bottles/) and [bulbs](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bulbs/) are available on our website for many current and recent models.

- How long should I leave my lava lamp on?







Most Mathmos lava lamps can be used for up to six hours at a time. Exceptions include the Mathmos [Candle lava lamps](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/candle-powered/), which should be used for no more than four hours, and the Mathmos [Saturn](https://mathmos.com/product/saturn-giant-lava-lamp/) and [Jumbo Jet](https://mathmos.com/product/jumbo-jet-giant-lava-lamp/) Giant lava lamps, which can be used for up to twelve hours. Always follow the operating instructions supplied with your lava lamp.

- Where can I find the Mathmos logo on my lava lamp?







The Mathmos logo can be found in several locations depending on the model, including the base, cap, cord label and, on most models, etched onto the bottle. Some bottles also feature the Mathmos name or logo moulded into the glass on the underside.

- Why does my lava lamp have specks or bubbles in the glass?







Mathmos lava lamp bottles are made in one of the highest quality automated glass factories in Great Britain. Small bubbles and specks in the glass are a normal part of the manufacturing process and are not a fault. They do not affect the performance, safety or lifespan of your lava lamp.

- Why is the wax in my lava lamp stuck at the top?







A small amount of wax at the top of your lava lamp bottle can occur as the lamp cools between uses and is not usually a cause for concern. If a large amount of wax remains stuck at the top during operation, the lamp may have overheated. Turn the lamp off and allow it to cool completely before using it again.

- Why is the liquid in my lava lamp cloudy?







A cloudy lava lamp bottle is usually caused by the bottle being shaken or moved while hot, or by the bottle reaching the end of its lifespan. If your lava lamp is cloudy, run it through a few normal operating cycles to see if the liquid clears. If the cloudiness does not improve, a [replacement bottle](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bottles/) may be required and can be purchased from our website.

- Why has my lava lamp bottle faded?







The colour of a lava lamp bottle can gradually fade over time due to age, heat and exposure to light. To help preserve the colour of your lava lamp, keep it away from direct sunlight, strong natural light and other heat sources. If your lava lamp bottle is still under warranty and you have followed the care instructions, please contact our [customer service team](https://mathmos.com/contact-us/). Otherwise, a [replacement bottle](https://mathmos.com/lava-lamps/spares-extras/lava-lamp-bottles/) can be purchased from our website.

- How does a lava lamp work?

Mathmos has refined its lava lamp formula since 1963 to improve clarity, colour stability and long-lasting flow.
